# **INVESTMENT THESIS COPY (VERBATIM)**

---

## **The Problem**

Precious metals are increasingly difficult to source through traditional mining.  
 Permitting timelines have extended, environmental liabilities have intensified, and many economically viable sites remain inaccessible due to regulatory constraints.

At the same time, the United States contains vast quantities of **above-ground material** — tailings, dump ore, legacy waste streams, and electronic waste — that hold recoverable precious metals but remain underutilized.

Traditional mining models are poorly suited to address this gap.

---

## **The Opportunity**

Breakthrough Management targets **already-disturbed material** rather than new extraction.

By focusing on:

* legacy tailings

* historic dump ore

* permitted reclamation sites

* urban and industrial waste streams

we reduce exploration risk while addressing environmental liabilities that regulators actively want resolved.

This approach aligns economic incentives with regulatory and community objectives.

---

## **The Strategy**

Our investment strategy is built around four principles:

### **1\. Permit First**

We prioritize projects where permitting, land access, and regulatory approvals are secured or well advanced. Capital is deployed into operational readiness, not entitlement risk.

### **2\. Proven Processing**

We partner with operators using validated, non-toxic processing technologies designed to meet modern environmental standards.

### **3\. Secured Offtake**

Projects are structured with downstream buyers and refining pathways identified early in the lifecycle to reduce commercialization risk.

### **4\. Asset Control**

Whenever possible, investments maintain exposure to physical assets and infrastructure, preserving downside protection and long-term optionality.

---

## **What We Avoid**

We intentionally avoid:

* greenfield exploration risk

* cyanide-dependent processing

* single-asset concentration

* unpermitted or speculative projects

Our focus is operational execution, not geological discovery.

---

## **Capital Discipline**

Breakthrough Management structures investments with an emphasis on:

* asset-level accountability

* operator co-investment

* defined reporting cadence

* institutional governance standards

Financial details and return structures are provided only to verified accredited investors through formal offering materials.

---

## **Measured Impact**

Each investment is evaluated on both:

* economic performance, and

* measurable environmental outcomes

This includes material diverted from landfills or tailings sites, site remediation progress, and compliance with third-party validation standards.
