1. Overview

The world is experiencing a critical materials crisis driven by the rise of artificial intelligence, electrification, and cloud infrastructure. AI data centers are now among the most resource-intensive facilities on earth — consuming enormous electricity, water, and metals — while at the same time producing the highest-grade electronic waste stream ever created.

Parallel to this, billions of dollars of gold and critical minerals remain trapped in abandoned mine waste piles across North America. Yet environmental regulations, toxic legacy processes, and permitting bottlenecks make processing this material nearly impossible.

In both sectors, the core failure is the same:

We have enormous reserves of accessible gold — but no responsible, affordable, or scalable way to extract it.

Minetek + Breakthrough Management offer the first vertically integrated, zero-waste, mobile metals recovery platform capable of extracting gold and critical metals directly at the source — safely, cleanly, and profitably.

2. The Problem Landscape
Problem 1: AI’s Explosive Growth Has Created a Resource Crisis

AI data centers consume extreme levels of power and water:

Up to 2 MW of power per facility — equal to ~2,000 homes

Millions of gallons of water annually

Local infrastructure upgrades paid by ratepayers

Heavy grid strain and rising utility costs

These facilities also generate massive volumes of server-grade PCBs, which contain:

Up to 1 gram of gold per board

Silver, palladium, platinum, copper

The world’s highest-grade “Urban Ore”

But current recycling systems are:

Too centralized

Too toxic

Too capacity-constrained

Poor at documentation and ESG traceability

Only 22% of global e-waste is formally recycled.

Impact:
Hyperscalers face rising ESG, regulatory, and reputational risks without a viable circular metals solution.

Problem 2: Traditional Mining Cannot Supply the Metals the AI Economy Requires

Remaining gold reserves in the ground are increasingly refractory — chemically locked in arsenic-bearing sulfides requiring:

Roasting

Pressure oxidation (POX)

Biological oxidation (BIOX)

These methods are:

Toxic

High-energy

Costly

Slow to permit

Environmentally hazardous

Deep earth mining is becoming less economic and more risky while demand for gold in electronics continues to rise.

Impact:
Traditional mining cannot scale to meet AI-driven metals demand.

Problem 3: Billions in Legacy Mine Waste Are Stranded and Unmonetized

Across the U.S.:

28,000+ abandoned mines

100+ million tons of dump ore in Colorado alone

As much as 10 gpt gold in some surface piles

Yet landowners cannot monetize this due to:

Tailings pond requirements

Toxic chemical constraints

High capital requirements

Permitting challenges

Liability risks

Transportation costs

Impact:
Waste that holds immense value remains untouched because existing extraction methods are incompatible with modern regulatory frameworks.

Problem 4: Logistics & Capital Requirements Make Centralized Processing Impossible

Both e-waste and dump ore suffer from the same structural barriers:

Transporting material is expensive and carbon-intensive

Centralized processing plants require millions in capital

Small and mid-sized operators cannot participate

ESG pressure demands decentralized, transparent solutions

Impact:
Without localized processing, the majority of accessible metals remain unrecovered.
